Red Beans and Rice

Description

Red beans and rice is a staple dish of New Orleans cuisine. As with most recipes around the world, there can be many variations of one dish depending on locale, time of year, family influence on traditions, to name a few. Generally, its main ingredients are red kidney beans, rice, and andouille sausage, a smoked pork sausage typically associated with Cajun cuisine. This is the way I ate it growing up in a Cajun family (now residing in Houston, Texas, but Cajun nonetheless). The dish itself is hearty, relatively spicy, and warms your heart and soul as it's consumed bite by bite. Depending on what meats are used, it can be smoky as well.

This specific recipe comes from one of my favorite chefs, J. Kenji Lopez-Alt. He goes to incredible lengths to keep recipes as authentic as possible but always provides multiple ways to achieve similar results if you don't have certain ingredients available to you or you don't have the time to devote to a recipe. I would highly recommend his cookbook, The Food Lab. It is a guide to understanding cooking better through a scientific lens. Many of his recipes are also available on Serious Eats, a food and drink blog and recipe resource.

Enough with the plugs, let's get to this recipe. It starts off with Cajun cooking's "holy trinity" of vegetables, onion, celery, and green bell pepper. Much like France's mirepoix (onion, celery, and carrot), it is the main flavor base of most Cajun cuisine, hence the holy trinity moniker. This is the flavor backbone of the dish, tying all the major flavors together subtly and over time as the dish simmers. Beyond that we have garlic, cayenne pepper, sage, black pepper, thyme, bay leaves, andouille sausage, among others that turn beans and rice into something otherworldly.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) red kidney beans
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) vegetable oil or lard
  • 1 pound (about 450g) cooked andouille sausage, cut into 1/2-inch disks
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped (about 12 ounces; 340g)
  • 1 green bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, and finely chopped (about 8 ounces; 225g)
  • 4 ribs celery, finely chopped (about 8 ounces; 225g)
  • 4 medium cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon (3 to 15g) ground cayenne pepper (depending on how hot you like it)
  • 1 teaspoon (about 4g) ground sage
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 smoked ham hock (optional but highly recommend)
  • 8 ounces (225g) pickled pork shoulder or rind (optional; see note)
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Hot sauce, such as Crystal or Frank's, to taste
  • Cider vinegar, to taste (optional; see note)
  • Cooled white rice, for serving
